Mapo Tofu Recipe

A favorite of mine, a mouth-watering spicy dish that uses tofu and ground pork. It is an iconic dish known for its red sauce and mala flavor. You will need a variety of ingredients but it will be well worth it in the end.

Ingredients:

  • One pack of medium tofu
  • 250g of ground pork (medium to lean)
  • Dobanjang (Spicy fermented bean paste)
  • Fermented black beans
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Soy sauce
  • Green Onions
  • Chinese peppercorns (red or green is fine)
  • Corn starch

Steps:

  1. Add peppercorns to a pan and heat on medium hot. We want to activate the peppercorn, it should begin to smell.
  2. Take peppercorn off heat after you can smell them. Grind into powder
  3. To your ground pork mix soy sauce, salt, sugar, and some of the grounded peppercorn
  4. Fry the ground pork until crispy. Set aside.
  5. Start a pot of water. While waitng for water to boil, cut tofu into 1-inch cubes
  6. Add tofu to boiling water. Let it cook for one minute, then strain. Be careful as tofu is delicate.
  7. Mince garlic, ginger, and the whites of green onion together. Then add dobanjang and fermented black beans and cut that up as well.
  8. Fry the spice paste with the leftover ground pork oil. Once fragrant, add water. Add a splash of soy sauce
  9. Once bubbling, add tofu and ground pork.
  10. Add corn starch slurry to thicken
  11. Add rest of peppercorn powder for finishing mala taste and green onion greens.
