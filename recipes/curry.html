Japanese Curry

Japanese curry is an imported product from the British colonies. Modern curry is actually super convenient. Bordering on stew, this is a highly customizable dish. The most important ingredient is the curry roux block which can be purchased at almost any large grocery store nowadays.

Ingredients

  • Japanese curry roux (Regular serving will only need 4 cubes)
  • Protein of choice (beef chunks, etc.)
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 medium carrot
  • 3 medium yellow potatos
  • 2 cloves of garlic

Steps

  1. Dice your onion and cut carrot and potatos into bite-sized chunks
  2. Mince garlic
  3. Cut meat into bit-sized chunks
  4. Heat up pan with oil and add onion, carrot, and potatos. Cook until onions are transparent
  5. Add meat and try to cook or brown
  6. Once meat is cooked, add 4 cups of water. Bring it to simmer
  7. In the meantime, cut up curry roux cubes for easier dissolving
  8. Once water is simmering, add roux into the water
  9. The mix will then thicken once roux has been added
  10. Serve with white rice or anything of your choice
